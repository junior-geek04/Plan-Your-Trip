BLOCKCHAINS IN LOGISTICS

Feb 18, 2018

In the simplest words if we try to answer the question about what a blockchain is?

The blockchain is a simple yet ingenious way of passing information from point A to point B in a fully automated and safe manner. As the name suggests, a blockchain consists of virtual blocks loaded with information. A block has 3 main parts-

1. Hash

2. Information

3. Hash of the previous block.

A hash is like a fingerprint of a block. It helps us to identify it. Information is the data stored in the block, which we need to transfer. Hash of the previous block is also stored in it which connects it from its previous block and makes a series of connected blocks a.k.a. blockchain.

Blockchain technology is considered by many to have a lot more applications and usage than it initially seems. Many Private and public companies, colleges and universities, and government agencies are all working quickly to leverage blockchain technology’s potential.

How is blockchain used in logistics? Every day logistics professionals have to navigate a plethora of options and “what if” scenarios when sending a truckload of goods across the country, all while documenting each step of the journey with detailed paperwork. The current process of moving goods from origin to destination is complicated and lacking an intelligent as well as secure source of information to store and track all transactions involved.

Advantages of blockchain technology in logistics:
1. Storage and security of all transactions
2.Improving efficiency of supply chain
3. Monitors performance
4. Improves payment and pricing process
5. Monitors real-time freight capacity
6. Detects frauds
7. Prevents thefts

The blockchain technology offers a solution to most current issues. There is a number of effective implementation strategies that are being tested now, and the industry is going to change forever as more organizations get involved.
